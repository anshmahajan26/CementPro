The Story of Smart-RMC

A simple guide explaining how the Manager in the office uses AI predictions to purchase cement and prevent silo depletion at the batching plant.

1. The Presentation Story (How the Roles Interact)

Explain this flow to your friends and the examiner. The system is split logically between office planning and physical plant loading:

Step 1: The Manager in the Central Office

The Manager Manager Role is responsible for inventory planning and forecasting. They do not drive concrete trucks or pour cement.

Instead, they sit in the office and run our AI demand forecast model. Based on project timelines and local weather inputs, the AI predicts how many cubic meters (m³) of Ready-Mix Concrete (RMC) the plant needs to supply over the coming week.

Based on this forecasted RMC demand, the system calculates the exact quantity of cement (in tonnes) required for the future RMC concrete mixes.

Step 2: Checking the Silo & Warning of Shortfalls

The Manager inputs their current silo level (e.g. 50 tonnes of cement in storage). The system runs a silo burn-down simulation.

It subtracts the cement required for future concrete batches day-by-day. If the inventory is projected to drop below zero, the system raises an actionable warning alert indicating a cement shortfall.

Step 3: Ordering Cement and Refilling the Silo

To prevent running out of cement, the Manager clicks "Dispatch Orders" only on the days where a shortfall is flagged. This triggers a cement refill order (a cement load purchase request) for that day's deficit.

This order goes directly to the Operator at the mixing plant.

Step 4: The Operator at the Plant Floor

The Operator Operator Role is at the batching plant. They monitor the inbound cement supply orders.

When the external cement delivery truck arrives at the plant, the Operator unloads the dry cement into the storage silo and updates the status to DELIVERED. The silo is now successfully replenished, and the plant is fully stocked to mix the future concrete packages!

2. Website Features & Page Walkthrough

1. Dashboard (Command Center)

Provides the executive summary. Calculates KPIs (predicted demand, cement requirements, next-day emissions) and plots them. Stacks three large charts vertically for maximum clarity:

  • Procurement vs Demand: A dual-bar chart showing forecasted RMC concrete volume and the corresponding cement needed.
  • Project Progress vs Demand: A lifecycle bar chart showing demand starting high in the early stages of a project and falling as the concrete structure is completed.
  • Carbon Output Metrics: A stacked area chart showing emissions from cement manufacturing, batching power, and truck travel.
  • Material Pollution Source: A donut pie chart showing that cement is the main driver of pollution, while aggregates contribute a minor fraction.

2. Cement Procurement Planning

Where the Manager performs the inventory burn-down calculations. Shows the day-by-day depletion curve of the cement silo and has a button to send purchase refill orders to the Operator when a shortfall occurs.

3. Forecast Modeling Station

Allows the Manager to input mix recipe criteria (like sand fractions, water-binder ratios, and slump ratings) and preview how changing these concrete variables shifts the predicted RMC demand curve.

4. Carbon Supply Chain Simulation

Allows the Manager to evaluate how switching to green cement blends (like LC3, fly-ash, or slag cement) reduces total emissions and improves the plant's overall sustainability rating.

3. Machine Learning Models & Algorithms

The Python FastAPI server runs four main forecasting algorithms:

  • XGBoost Regressor: A tree model that builds decision trees sequentially. Each new tree corrects the prediction mistakes made by the previous trees. It is highly accurate for complex non-linear cement demand values.
  • RandomForest Regressor: An ensemble model that trains multiple decision trees independently and averages their predictions to prevent errors (overfitting).
  • LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory): A sequence-based neural network model with memory cells that learns historical time-series patterns (e.g. week-on-week demand curves).
  • Linear Regression: A basic statistical model used as a simple baseline to evaluate the complexity benefits of XGBoost and LSTM.

4. Mathematical Models & Formulas

1. Hybrid Model Blend
ŷ_hybrid = (0.65 * ŷ_XGB) + (0.35 * ŷ_RF)
Combines XGBoost and RandomForest predictions using validation-optimized weights (65% / 35%).
2. Weather-Adjusted Demand Factor
WF = 1.0 - rain_factor - temperature_extreme_factor
ŷ_adjusted = ŷ_hybrid * WF
Rain and extreme cold/heat limits the capacity to pour concrete on site. The Open-Meteo weather API helps adjust the RMC demand downward if severe rain is forecast.
3. Concrete Lifecycle Decay Equation
Demand(d) = Raw_Demand * (1.25 - ((d - d_min) / (d_max - d_min)) * 1.05)
Scales RMC demand to start high (1.25x) during foundation works and drop linearly to a baseline (0.20x) as the project concludes.
4. Supply Chain Carbon Emissions Breakdown
Total_Emissions(t) = E_cement(t) + E_batching(t) + E_transport(t)
- E_cement = cement_kg * blend_factor (OPC = 0.92, PPC = 0.72, PSC = 0.65, LC3 = 0.55).
- E_batching = (demand / truck_capacity) * batching_time * 0.8 + demand * 37.
- E_transport = (demand / truck_capacity) * transport_time * 2.5 + demand * 48.

5. Glossary of Concrete Mix Terms (Single-Line Explanations)

cement_kg_m3
The weight of cement in kg needed to make 1 cubic meter of concrete; it acts as the primary binder glue.
aggregate_10mm_pct
The percentage of medium-sized (10mm) gravel stones; used to fill the voids between larger rocks in the mix.
aggregate_20mm_pct
The percentage of coarse (20mm) stones; forms the structural skeleton that gives concrete its heavy compression strength.
agg_moisture_content_pct
The percentage of water already trapped inside aggregate piles; must be measured to avoid adding too much extra water.
water_binder_ratio
The weight of water divided by the weight of cement; a lower ratio gives stronger concrete, while a higher ratio makes it weaker.
slump_mm
A physical height test measuring how wet/fluid concrete is; higher slump means it flows easily, lower slump means it is stiff.
